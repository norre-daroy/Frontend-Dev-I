Rule of Thirds

Vessi

vessi.com

Rule of Thirds refers to a 3 by 3 grid, equally shaped blocks on top of an image or layout. The points where the grid lines intersect are referred to as eye-catching points.

The hero image of Vessi website fall into the eye-catching points. Moreover, rule of thirds is also evident when viewing the website in “medium view”, the person holding the umbrella falls into “third” region where the eye is naturally drawn at first glance.

PARC: Contrast

Harvard

harvard.edu

Contrast refers to the difference of any design element that makes one item of the design as dominant. Contrast is a requirement for accessibility but can be used as a design principle in general.

The hero image of Harvard website shows contrast by the choice and manipulation of its colors. Its header has a light colored text over a dark overlay image. This concept attracts viewers interest and easier for users to make sense of the visual.

Fitt's Law

Etsy

etsy.com

Fitt's Law refers to distance from one element to and size of the target. This means that the touch targets should be large enough or close enough for users to accurately select them.

Etsy website follows this principle by positioning interactive elements for their fresh finds promotion close to each other reducing selection time. Moreover, the interactive elements enlarges when user hovers over them making it easier to click.
